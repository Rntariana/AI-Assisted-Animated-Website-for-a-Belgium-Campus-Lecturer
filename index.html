// WHO IS IKRAAM SADEK ✕

Junior Lecturer at Belgium Campus iTversity, South Africa — passionate about making programming education engaging, interactive, and genuinely useful beyond the classroom.

Rather than a one-sided lecture, Ikraam believes the best learning happens through open class discussions. He brings energy, adaptability, and the attitude that "We'll make it work."

His go-to advice: after receiving feedback on a practical project, apply it — and publish your work on GitHub. Let your portfolio show companies what you can do in practice, not just in theory.

I have always been driven by a passion for problem-solving and a genuine desire to help others navigate complex challenges. My career path led me to education — where I found that teaching is the most impactful way to share knowledge and lift others up. I joined Belgium Campus iTversity as a Junior Lecturer, and it has been an incredibly rewarding journey.
Across my career, I have developed expertise in software development, web programming, and application design. I hold an Advanced Diploma in Application Development from Rosebank College. I currently teach 11 modules spanning first and second-year level, covering programming, web development, software analysis, software testing, and information systems. My approach is always discussion-based and interactive — because I believe the best learning happens in conversation, not in isolation.
My priority as an educator is to ensure students leave with practical, demonstrable skills. I always encourage students to apply their feedback, build real projects, and publish them on GitHub — because a live portfolio speaks louder than any exam result. My signature approach is simple: "We'll make it work." Whatever the challenge, we find a way through it together.
